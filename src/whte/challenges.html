Statistics Challenges

The Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York city has hired you to determine whether its new acquisitions of Latin American beadwork is authentic. Your job is to create a simulation of a known authentic original, and then compare the variance of color in the simulation to the new acquisitions.

  1. The first column of your simulation is complete. Its color range is narrow (0 to 20). You will need to add two or three more columns, and a wider range of colors, to make it a better match to the museum’s sample. Don't forget to save your work, and write down the color range you used.

    Launch the Software!

  2. Now that you know the color range, you can calculate the variance. When you have a set of consecutive integers, the mean is (smallest + largest)/2. You can estimate the variance for the new color range you created using the formula for the estimate of the mean of your random numbers, along with the following project:

    Launch the Software!

  3. This program already calculates the sum of the squares. Add the missing block to help it calculate the variance.

  4. Use the following steps to calculate the variance from a set of values:

    1. Calculate the mean of the sample (using (smallest + largest)/2).
    2. Subtract the mean from each data point. ↓
    3. Square each result. ↓
    4. Find the sum of the squared values. ↓
    5. Divide by n - 1, where n is the number of data points.
